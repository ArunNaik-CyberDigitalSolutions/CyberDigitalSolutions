### 2. Vendor Risk Management, VRMMM & Supply Chain Cyber Risk Assessment

**Repo:** [`vendor-risk-vrmmm-supply-chain-assessment`](https://github.com/ArunNaik-CyberDigitalSolutions/vendor-risk-vrmmm-supply-chain-assessment)

A unified assessment combining **Vendor Risk Management (VRM) control evaluation**, **Vendor Risk Management Maturity Model (VRMMM)** scoring, and **Supply Chain Cyber Risk Management** — covering the full spectrum of third-party, nth-party, and supply chain cyber exposure across the entire vendor lifecycle.

This assessment recognises that modern cyber risk does not stop at the direct vendor relationship. Nth-party risk — the suppliers, platforms, and service providers behind your vendors — represents one of the fastest-growing and least-visible sources of enterprise cyber exposure.

---

**Three Integrated Assessment Lenses:**

| Lens | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 🔵 VRM Control Assessment | Control effectiveness across the vendor lifecycle | Risk-rated vendor control scorecard |
| 🟠 VRMMM Maturity Assessment | Organisational maturity in managing vendor risk | Current state → target state maturity profile |
| 🔴 Supply Chain Cyber Risk | Third-party, nth-party, and supply chain exposure | Supply chain risk register and concentration analysis |

---

**Vendor Tier Classification:**

A foundational input to all three lenses is vendor tiering — classifying vendors by their data access, operational dependency, and supply chain reach:

| Tier | Label | Description | Assessment Depth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Critical | Direct access to sensitive data or critical systems; operational dependency | Full assessment — all lenses |
| Tier 2 | High | Significant access or dependency; some operational impact if disrupted | VRM + VRMMM assessment |
| Tier 3 | Moderate | Limited data access; manageable disruption impact | VRM control assessment |
| Tier 4 | Low | Minimal access and low operational dependency | Baseline screening only |

---

**Lens 1 — VRM Control Assessment**

Evaluates the effectiveness of cybersecurity controls across the full vendor lifecycle for direct (first-party) vendors:

| Domain | Description |
|---|---|
| Vendor Identification & Classification | Inventory completeness, tiering accuracy, and business context |
| Pre-Engagement Due Diligence | Security questionnaires, certifications, financial health, and regulatory standing |
| Contractual & Regulatory Controls | Security clauses, SLAs, right-to-audit, data handling, and breach notification obligations |
| Cybersecurity Controls Assessment | Vendor-side control maturity — policies, access management, patching, encryption, incident response |
| Ongoing Monitoring & Assurance | Periodic reassessment cadence, continuous monitoring triggers, certification renewals |
| Incident & Breach Management | Vendor notification obligations, joint response protocols, escalation paths |
| Concentration & Dependency Risk | Single-vendor exposure, geographic concentration, platform dependency |
| Offboarding & Exit Management | Data return and destruction, access revocation, transition risk and continuity planning |

---

**Lens 2 — VRMMM Maturity Assessment**

Evaluates the organisation's capability maturity in managing vendor risk — not just whether controls exist, but how consistently, strategically, and repeatably they are applied:

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Initial | Ad hoc, unstructured — vendor risk managed reactively with no formal program |
| Level 2 | Developing | Basic processes exist but are inconsistently applied across vendor relationships |
| Level 3 | Defined | Documented, standardised VRM processes consistently applied organisation-wide |
| Level 4 | Managed | Quantitatively measured, risk-informed — decisions driven by data and metrics |
| Level 5 | Optimised | Continuously improving — VRM integrated into enterprise risk strategy and supply chain governance |

---

**Lens 3 — Supply Chain Cyber Risk Assessment**

Extends VRM beyond direct vendors to assess **third-party, nth-party, and supply chain cyber risk** — mapping dependencies, identifying concentration, and evaluating systemic exposure:

**Third-Party Risk (Direct Vendors):**

| Assessment Area | Description |
|---|---|
| Security Posture Assessment | Control maturity of direct vendors via questionnaire, attestation, or audit |
| Data Flow Mapping | What data is shared, how it flows, where it resides within vendor environments |
| Access & Privilege Review | Vendor access to systems, networks, and sensitive assets — scope and controls |
| Regulatory & Compliance Alignment | Vendor adherence to relevant regulatory requirements (Privacy Act, IRAP, ISO 27001, SOC 2) |
| Business Continuity & Resilience | Vendor BCP/DR capability and tested recovery capacity |

**Nth-Party Risk (Vendors' Vendors):**

| Assessment Area | Description |
|---|---|
| Nth-Party Discovery | Identifying the subcontractors, cloud platforms, and service providers behind your direct vendors |
| Dependency Mapping | Visualising the supply chain beyond Tier 1 to uncover hidden dependencies and shared infrastructure |
| Nth-Party Concentration Risk | Multiple vendors using the same cloud provider, software platform, or data centre — systemic exposure |
| Contractual Flow-Down Controls | Whether security obligations flow down from vendor to their subcontractors and nth parties |
| Nth-Party Incident Impact Assessment | Evaluating how an nth-party failure or breach could propagate through the supply chain to your organisation |

**Supply Chain Risk Domains:**

| Domain | Third-Party View | Nth-Party View |
|---|---|---|
| Governance & Ownership | VRM policy coverage of direct vendors | Policy flow-down requirements to subcontractors |
| Vendor Inventory & Mapping | Direct vendor register completeness | Nth-party discovery and dependency mapping |
| Security Controls Assessment | Direct vendor control evaluation | Nth-party control visibility and attestation |
| Concentration Risk | Single-vendor dependency exposure | Shared infrastructure and platform concentration |
| Incident & Breach Exposure | Direct vendor breach notification | Nth-party incident propagation risk |
| Contractual Coverage | Direct vendor security clauses | Subcontractor and flow-down obligation coverage |
| Regulatory Compliance | Vendor compliance with applicable regulations | Nth-party compliance visibility and obligations |
| Exit & Transition Risk | Direct vendor exit management | Supply chain continuity on nth-party disruption |

---

**Combined Assessment Domain Summary:**

| Domain | VRM Control View | VRMMM Maturity View | Supply Chain View |
|---|---|---|---|
| Governance & Policy | VRM strategy ownership and policy existence | Maturity of governance structures and accountability | Supply chain risk policy and flow-down requirements |
| Vendor Identification & Tiering | Inventory completeness and classification accuracy | Consistency and rigour of tiering methodology | Nth-party discovery and dependency mapping |
| Due Diligence & Onboarding | Pre-contract assessment thoroughness | Repeatability and standardisation of onboarding | Third and nth-party pre-engagement screening |
| Contractual Controls | Security clause coverage and enforceability | Maturity of contract management processes | Subcontractor flow-down obligation coverage |
| Ongoing Monitoring & Assurance | Frequency and depth of ongoing reviews | Proactive vs reactive monitoring capability | Continuous nth-party exposure monitoring |
| Incident & Breach Management | Notification and joint response obligations | Maturity of vendor incident integration | Nth-party incident propagation and response |
| Offboarding & Exit Management | Secure termination and data return controls | Consistency and completeness of exit processes | Supply chain continuity on vendor exit |
| Concentration & Dependency Risk | Exposure identification and treatment | Strategic management of supply chain dependencies | Systemic nth-party and platform concentration risk |

---

- Vendor tier classification register (Tier 1–4)

- VRMMM maturity scoring — current state vs target state per domain
- Third-party risk assessment outputs per vendor
- Nth-party discovery register and dependency map

- Integrated gap heat map across all three lenses
- Contractual flow-down coverage assessment
- Prioritised uplift initiatives — quick wins vs strategic investments

### 4. NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 Assessment

**Repo:** [`nist-csf2-assessment`](https://github.com/ArunNaik-CyberDigitalSolutions/nist-csf2-assessment)

A **function-level maturity assessment** aligned to the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 (CSF 2.0)**, released February 2024. This updated assessment covers all **six core functions** and 22 categories — including the newly introduced **Govern** function, which elevates cybersecurity risk management to an organisational governance priority.

**Six Core Functions (CSF 2.0):**

| Function | Categories | Focus | New in 2.0? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🟤 Govern | 6 | Organisational context, risk strategy, roles, policy, oversight, supply chain risk | ✅ New |
| 🔵 Identify | 5 | Asset management, risk assessment, improvement | Updated |
| 🟢 Protect | 5 | Identity management, awareness, data security, platform security, resilience | Updated |
| 🟡 Detect | 2 | Continuous monitoring, adverse event analysis | Updated |
| 🟠 Respond | 4 | Incident management, analysis, mitigation, reporting | Updated |
| 🔴 Recover | 3 | Incident recovery, communication, restoration | Updated |

**Govern Function — Category Detail (New in CSF 2.0):**

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| GV.OC — Organisational Context | Understanding the organisation's mission, stakeholders, legal/regulatory obligations |
| GV.RM — Risk Management Strategy | Risk appetite, tolerance, priorities, and senior leadership-established strategy |
| GV.RR — Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities | Cybersecurity roles defined, understood, and communicated across the organisation |
| GV.PO — Policy | Cybersecurity policy established, communicated, and enforced |
| GV.OV — Oversight | Results of cybersecurity risk management feed into senior leadership review |
| GV.SC — Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management | Supply chain risk management integrated into broader cyber risk processes |

**Maturity Tiers:**

| Tier | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Partial | Ad hoc, reactive, limited risk awareness |
| Tier 2 | Risk Informed | Some awareness and intent, not organisation-wide |
| Tier 3 | Repeatable | Defined, consistent, and formally approved processes |
| Tier 4 | Adaptive | Dynamic, continuously improving, integrated risk-informed decisions |

**What it delivers:**
- Function-level current vs target state scoring across all six functions
- Govern function maturity profile (new dedicated output)
- Category-level gap analysis
- Tier-based maturity positioning
- Prioritised uplift roadmap
- Executive radar chart summary across all six functions
- Supply chain risk maturity view (aligned to GV.SC)

**Intended users:** Security architects, CISOs, risk officers, cyber BAs, board-level reporting, government and regulated industries
